# How Stock Market Works: Understanding Market Mechanics

## Introduction

The stock market can seem complex, but understanding how it works is essential for successful investing. This guide will help you understand the mechanics of stock market operations, from basic concepts to advanced trading mechanisms.

## Stock Market Fundamentals

### What is the Stock Market?
The stock market is a collection of markets where stocks (shares) are bought and sold. It provides a platform for companies to raise capital and for investors to buy and sell securities.

### Key Functions
- **Capital Formation**: Helps companies raise capital
- **Price Discovery**: Determines fair prices for securities
- **Liquidity**: Provides liquidity to investors
- **Risk Management**: Allows risk transfer
- **Economic Growth**: Contributes to economic growth

### Market Participants

#### Companies
- **Issuers**: Companies that issue shares
- **Purpose**: Raise capital for growth and expansion
- **Benefits**: Access to capital, public profile
- **Responsibilities**: Disclosure, corporate governance
- **Examples**: Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank

#### Investors
- **Individual Investors**: Retail investors
- **Institutional Investors**: Mutual funds, insurance companies
- **Foreign Investors**: Foreign institutional investors
- **Purpose**: Investment returns, portfolio diversification
- **Impact**: Market liquidity and price movements

#### Intermediaries
- **Stock Brokers**: Facilitate trading
- **Depositories**: Hold securities in electronic form
- **Clearing Houses**: Ensure trade settlement
- **Investment Bankers**: Help companies raise capital
- **Analysts**: Provide research and recommendations

## Stock Market Structure

### Primary Market
The primary market is where companies issue new securities to raise capital.

#### Initial Public Offering (IPO)
- **Definition**: First time a company offers shares to public
- **Process**: Company files prospectus, SEBI approval, public subscription
- **Purpose**: Raise capital, provide exit to early investors
- **Participants**: Company, investment bankers, investors
- **Pricing**: Based on company valuation and market conditions

#### Follow-on Public Offering (FPO)
- **Definition**: Additional share issue by already listed company
- **Purpose**: Raise additional capital
- **Types**: Rights issue, public issue
- **Pricing**: Based on market price
- **Impact**: Dilution of existing shareholders

#### Rights Issue
- **Definition**: Issue of shares to existing shareholders
- **Ratio**: Based on existing holdings
- **Price**: Usually at discount to market price
- **Purpose**: Raise capital without diluting ownership
- **Advantages**: Maintains ownership structure

### Secondary Market
The secondary market is where existing securities are traded among investors.

#### Stock Exchanges
- **Definition**: Organized market for securities trading
- **Function**: Provide platform for trading
- **Examples**: NSE, BSE
- **Features**: Electronic trading, price discovery
- **Regulation**: Regulated by SEBI

#### Over-the-Counter (OTC) Market
- **Definition**: Trading outside exchanges
- **Function**: Trading of unlisted securities
- **Features**: Less regulated, higher risk
- **Participants**: Institutional investors, high-net-worth individuals
- **Examples**: Unlisted companies, debt instruments

## Trading Mechanism

### Order Types

#### Market Order
- **Definition**: Order to buy/sell at current market price
- **Execution**: Immediate execution at best available price
- **Advantages**: Guaranteed execution
- **Disadvantages**: Price uncertainty
- **Suitability**: When execution is more important than price

#### Limit Order
- **Definition**: Order to buy/sell at specified price or better
- **Execution**: Only when price meets limit
- **Advantages**: Price control
- **Disadvantages**: No execution guarantee
- **Suitability**: When price is more important than execution

#### Stop Loss Order
- **Definition**: Order to sell when price falls below specified level
- **Purpose**: Limit losses
- **Execution**: Converts to market order when triggered
- **Advantages**: Automatic loss protection
- **Disadvantages**: May trigger at inopportune time

#### Stop Limit Order
- **Definition**: Combination of stop loss and limit order
- **Purpose**: Limit losses with price control
- **Execution**: Becomes limit order when stop price reached
- **Advantages**: Price and loss protection
- **Disadvantages**: Complex, may not execute

### Trading Process

#### Order Placement
1. **Login**: Login to trading platform
2. **Select Stock**: Choose stock to trade
3. **Order Type**: Select order type
4. **Quantity**: Specify quantity
5. **Price**: Specify price (if limit order)
6. **Place Order**: Submit order

#### Order Matching
- **Price-Time Priority**: Orders matched based on price and time
- **Buy Orders**: Matched with sell orders
- **Sell Orders**: Matched with buy orders
- **Execution**: Orders executed when match found
- **Confirmation**: Trade confirmation sent

#### Trade Settlement
- **T+2 Settlement**: Trade settled in 2 working days
- **Demat Account**: Shares transferred to demat account
- **Bank Account**: Funds transferred to/from bank account
- **Confirmation**: Settlement confirmation received
- **Brokerage**: Brokerage charges applied

## Price Discovery Mechanism

### Supply and Demand
- **Demand**: Buyers willing to buy at various prices
- **Supply**: Sellers willing to sell at various prices
- **Equilibrium**: Price where demand equals supply
- **Dynamic**: Constantly changing based on new information
- **Efficiency**: Market efficiency in price discovery

### Factors Affecting Stock Prices

#### Company-Specific Factors
- **Financial Performance**: Revenue, profit, growth
- **Management Quality**: Leadership, strategy
- **Products/Services**: Innovation, competitive advantage
- **Market Position**: Market share, brand value
- **Future Prospects**: Growth potential, expansion plans

#### Economic Factors
- **GDP Growth**: Economic growth rate
- **Inflation**: Price level changes
- **Interest Rates**: Cost of borrowing
- **Exchange Rates**: Currency values
- **Employment**: Job market conditions

#### Market Factors
- **Market Sentiment**: Investor psychology
- **Market Trends**: Bull and bear markets
- **Sector Performance**: Sector-specific trends
- **Global Markets**: International market influence
- **Regulatory Changes**: New regulations, policies

#### Political Factors
- **Government Policies**: Economic policies, reforms
- **Political Stability**: Government stability
- **International Relations**: Trade policies, sanctions
- **Elections**: Election outcomes and expectations
- **Geopolitical Events**: Wars, conflicts, crises

## Market Indices

### Purpose of Indices
- **Benchmark**: Measure market performance
- **Indicator**: Economic indicator
- **Reference**: Reference for fund performance
- **Trading**: Basis for index-based products
- **Research**: Market research tool

### Major Indian Indices

#### Sensex (BSE Sensex)
- **Composition**: 30 largest companies by market cap
- **Base Year**: 1978-79
- **Base Value**: 100
- **Calculation**: Free-float market capitalization weighted
- **Significance**: Represents Indian economy

#### Nifty 50 (NSE Nifty)
- **Composition**: 50 largest companies by market cap
- **Base Year**: 1995
- **Base Value**: 1000
- **Calculation**: Free-float market capitalization weighted
- **Significance**: Represents Indian equity market

#### Other Indices
- **Nifty Next 50**: Next 50 large companies
- **Nifty Midcap 100**: Mid-cap companies
- **Nifty Smallcap 100**: Small-cap companies
- **Sectoral Indices**: Banking, IT, Pharma indices
- **International Indices**: Dow Jones, S&P 500, FTSE

### Index Calculation

#### Market Capitalization Weighted
- **Formula**: Σ (Price × Shares) / Base Value × Base Index
- **Free Float**: Shares available for trading
- **Adjustments**: Adjustments for corporate actions
- **Rebalancing**: Periodic rebalancing of constituents
- **Divisor**: Base divisor for calculation

## Trading Sessions and Timings

### Regular Trading Session
- **Pre-Open Session**: 9:00 AM - 9:15 AM
- **Regular Session**: 9:15 AM - 3:30 PM
- **Post-Close Session**: 3:40 PM - 4:00 PM
- **Trading Days**: Monday to Friday (except holidays)
- **Settlement**: T+2 settlement cycle

### Pre-Open Session
- **Purpose**: Price discovery before market opens
- **Order Collection**: Collect orders before opening
- **Price Determination**: Determine opening price
- **Order Matching**: Match orders at opening price
- **Volatility Control**: Control opening volatility

### Post-Close Session
- **Purpose**: After-hours trading
- **Order Placement**: Place orders after market close
- **Price Discovery**: Determine closing price
- **Order Matching**: Match orders at closing price
- **Next Day**: Orders executed next day

## Market Participants and Their Roles

### Retail Investors
- **Definition**: Individual investors
- **Characteristics**: Small investment amounts
- **Impact**: Market liquidity and sentiment
- **Behavior**: Often influenced by market sentiment
- **Challenges**: Limited resources, information asymmetry

### Institutional Investors
- **Definition**: Large organizations investing in securities
- **Types**: Mutual funds, insurance companies, pension funds
- **Characteristics**: Large investment amounts
- **Impact**: Market movements, price discovery
- **Advantages**: Professional management, research capabilities

### Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs)
- **Definition**: Foreign institutions investing in Indian markets
- **Types**: Foreign mutual funds, pension funds, hedge funds
- **Impact**: Significant market impact
- **Regulation**: SEBI regulations for FIIs
- **Market Influence**: Market trends and sentiment

### Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs)
- **Definition**: Indian institutional investors
- **Types**: Mutual funds, insurance companies, banks
- **Characteristics**: Large investment amounts
- **Impact**: Market stability and liquidity
- **Growth**: Growing influence in Indian markets

## Market Psychology and Behavior

### Market Sentiment
- **Definition**: Overall attitude of investors
- **Indicators**: Market breadth, volatility, trading volume
- **Impact**: Short-term price movements
- **Measurement**: Sentiment indices, surveys
- **Influence**: Media, economic news, global events

### Behavioral Biases
- **Herd Mentality**: Following crowd behavior
- **Overconfidence**: Overestimating knowledge
- **Loss Aversion**: Fear of losses more than gains
- **Confirmation Bias**: Seeking confirming information
- **Recency Bias**: Overweighting recent events

### Market Cycles
- **Bull Market**: Rising prices, optimism
- **Bear Market**: Falling prices, pessimism
- **Market Top**: Peak of bull market
- **Market Bottom**: Low point of bear market
- **Recovery**: Recovery from bear market
